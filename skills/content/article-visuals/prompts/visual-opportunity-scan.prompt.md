# Visual Opportunity Scan Prompt

```text
Read this article or source draft and identify the best opportunities for HTML visual aids.

Source:
<paste or reference source>

Audience:
<target reader or talk audience>

Publishing surface:
<blog | docs site | talk page | workshop | other>

Analyze the source before proposing visuals.

Return:
1. The moments where the reader is most likely to need a visual explanation.
2. A ranked list of infographic or animated-component ideas.
3. For each idea, the teaching job, why it improves the article, and whether it should be static, animated, or interactive.
4. Any outside examples, public references, product screenshots, or visual conventions worth researching before building.
5. The smallest first visual to build and the browser checks that would prove it works.

Prefer visuals that explain sequence, state, flow, contrast, hierarchy, scale, or before/after change. Do not propose decorative art unless it directly improves comprehension.
```
